Web Accessibility Policy



Our Mission

This place looks familiar is committed to making our website accessible to everyone, including individuals with disabilities. By making our website accessible, we can reach a wider audience and provide better customer service to all of our users.



Conformance Standards

Our website will confirm to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2 by W3C. We will ensure that all new content, features, and functionality on our website meet these standards, and we will work to make our existing content accessible as well. Here are some examples of our implementations to meet the standards.
  1. 1. Clear and concise content: sentences and paragraphs are written short and clear; Images, illustrations, video, audio, and symbols are used to help clarify meaning.
  2. 2. Meaningful link text: link texts are straightforward to describe the content of the link target clearly.
  3. 3. Sufficient contrast between foreground and background: this includes text on images, background gradients, buttons, and other elements.
  4. 4. Clear and consistent navigation: navigation across pages within this website has consistent naming, styling, and positioning.
  5. 5. Interactive elements are easy to identify: the color of links on mouse hover is different.
  6. 6. Provide different viewport sizes: how page information is presented in different-sized viewports, such as mobile phones or zoomed browser windows is different. The position and presentation of main elements, such as the header and navigation are changed to make the best use of the space. The text size and line width are set to maximize readability and legibility.

Monitoring

  • This place looks familiar will review our accessibility policy weekly to ensure that we are meeting our commitment to accessibility. We will also conduct regular accessibility testing on our website using automated tools and manual testing methods to identify and address any accessibility barriers..


Audit policy

This place looks familiar will identify the pages to be tested, and define the criteria for a successful audit according to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG). We will use a web accessibility testing tool, Wave to scan the pages identified in the scope. We will record the results of each test and identify any accessibility defects found.
