Doll Factory (Upcoming...)

Based on the bestselling novel with the same title by Elizabeth Macneal, “The Doll Factory” is an upcoming TV series currently being shot around Dublin.

It tells the story of Iris who paints dolls’ faces for a living with her sister. But she aspires for something greater, she wants to be an artist and freely express herself on a canvas. When she meets pre- Raphaelite artist Louis Frost, and he asks her to model for him, she agrees, on condition that he teaches her to paint professionally.

The Doll Factory is a story about art, obsession and possession that is sure to grip you to your screen.

Paramount+ commissioned this TV series expected to be released in 2025. Amongst the cast is Esmé Creed-Miles (“Hanna”) as Iris, Éanna Hardwicke (“Normal People”) as Silas, Mirren Mack (“The Witcher: Blood Origin”) as Rose, and Saoirse Monica Jackson (“Derry Girls”).

Buliding in the series

Location: 2 Westland Row, Dublin 2

Among the scenes being shot in Dublin, we had access to exclusive information to where the murder scene had been shot. Right next to Pearse station, in 2 Westland Row, in the beginning of March the street was closed off and they shot the murder scene under the bridge. Since the story is set in Victorian London, they covered the pavement in fake cobblestone. The location chosen has naturally low natural light as there are no street lights under the bridge, so it’s perfect to capture such a scene.

We are looking forward to seeing how it turns out!
